A Glimpse into the World of Robotics

What is a robot?

Robot: An embodied agent that can be programmed to carry out a complex series of actions. A robot is an autonomous or partially autonomous machine capable of sensing its environment and making decisions based on computations. There are a variety of robots with unique qualities and capabilities.

To compute, this involves everything from small electronic circuits to multicore processors or even a cluster of networked computers. In action, robots vary the most, as some robots can move around, manipulate objects, perform specific tasks, while others can do many things.

Robots Typically Do 3 Things

Sense

Robots gather information from their surroundings using sensors.

Think

Robots process data and make decisions based on programming or AI algorithms.

Act

Robots perform actions using actuators like motors, arms, or wheels.

Robotics as a Field of Study

Robotics is a young and continuously growing interdisciplinary field. It involves the development and construction of systems that combine electrical and mechanical components with a computer for calculations. This includes the creation of machines that assist humans with repetitive, precise, or dangerous tasks. Robotics includes core disciplines such as electrical engineering, mechanical engineering, and computer science, but also intertwines with artificial intelligence, computer vision, applied mathematics, etc.

Goals and Applications

Robotics can be organized based on their applications. As mentioned above, robots are useful for completing tasks that require a lot of precision, repetitive and monotonous work. Robots can be found in manufacturing industries for welding, material handling, painting, assembling, picking, packing, and palletizing, etc. Similarly, robots can be found in agriculture for repetitive tasks like seeding, harvesting, weed control, optimal soil detection. Another common place to see robots in action is in healthcare, helping doctors with performing surgical procedures and providing therapy for patients. On the other hand, robots have revolutionized exploration, as robots can explore places where humans cannot get to easily, like the depths of the ocean or outer space. Moreover, other sectors where robots are useful are entertainment, customer service, food preparation, security, etc.
