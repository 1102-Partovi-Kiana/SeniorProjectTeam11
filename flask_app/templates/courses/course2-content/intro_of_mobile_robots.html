Introduction of Mobile robotics

Mobile robotics demands a combination of various disciplines, including computer science, engineering, artificial intelligence, social and cognitive sciences, psychology, mathematics, etc. Mobile robots are computers that perceive the external environment through its sensors and are able to perform certain tasks, while autonomous mobile robots are intelligent agents that are able to do complex tasks by identifying features, detecting patterns from previous experiences. Autonomous robots follow the characteristics of the old concept of robots, an agent or machine that mimics living beings like animals or humans. The core focuses of mobile robotics are locomotion, perception, cognition and navigation. With a wide range of practical applications, these devices have made significant contributions to human social, agricultural, technical, and economic progress.

Let's Dive In

Agent - A Technical Term

An agent is an entity that produces an effect. Robots are physical machines that complete tasks that create an effect, therefore, in this context, a robot is an agent.

Autonomy - A Technical Term

In the context of robotics, autonomy refers to having power of self-government and undertaking tasks without outside interference.
