About the Autonomous Car

Our Autonomous Car Robot module introduces the fundamentals of self-driving technology. With sensors and constraints this robot is built to simulate real-world navigation and decision-making scenarios. Students can program the car to follow paths, avoid obstacles, to respond traffic-like environments.

Sensors and Navigation

The Autonomous Car Robot uses a combination of sensors and conditional logic to understand its environment. The sensor data is used to guide the robot’s movement, avoid collisions, and stay on track. It’s a hands-on way to explore how self-driving cars “see” the world and make movement decisions accordingly.

Decision-Making and Logic

This module focuses on how autonomous vehicles make choices. Through the exercises, conditional logic along with path planning will be used.

Real-World Task Simulation

The Autonomous Car Robots concepts can be applied to real-world autonomous vehicles. From navigating busy streets to making quick decisions in unexpected situations, this robot aids in the understanding of how the same principles are used in actual autonomous vehicles.
