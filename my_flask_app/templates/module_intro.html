Introduction to Robotics Programming

This module covers the basics of robotics programming, including the anatomy of robots, simulations, and foundational programming concepts.

What is a robot?

Robot: An embodied agent that can be programmed to carry out a complex series of actions. A robot is an autonomous or partially autonomous machine capable of sensing its environment and making decisions based on computations. There are a variety of robots with unique qualities and capabilities.

To compute, this involves everything from small electronic circuits to multicore processors or even a cluster of networked computers. In action, robots vary the most, as some robots can move around, manipulate objects, perform specific tasks, while others can do many things.

Understanding Robot Anatomy

Robotics is a young and continuously growing interdisciplinary field. It involves the development and construction of systems that combine electrical and mechanical components with a computer for calculations. This includes the creation of machines that assist humans with repetitive, precise, or dangerous tasks. Robotics includes core disciplines such as electrical engineering, mechanical engineering, and computer science, but also intertwines with artificial intelligence, computer vision, applied mathematics, etc.

  • Actuators: Motors that create movement.
  • Sensors: Devices that allow the robot to perceive its environment (e.g., cameras, touch sensors).
  • Controllers: The "brain" of the robot that processes inputs and sends commands to actuators.
  • Power Systems: Components that supply energy to the robot, like batteries.

Simulation Environments

Simulation environments allow developers to test and train robots in virtual settings, which is crucial for minimizing risks and optimizing designs before building physical robots.

Some popular simulation platforms include:

  • MuJoCo: A physics-based engine for simulating robots and dynamic systems.
  • OpenAI Gym: A toolkit for developing and comparing reinforcement learning algorithms, providing various robotics environments.

Using simulations, you can safely experiment with different robot behaviors and algorithms.

                
import gym
env = gym.make('FetchReach-v1')
env.reset()

Basic Programming for Robots

In this section, we’ll go through the basics of coding for robots. Here’s a simple code snippet to get started:

                
import gym

# Create a simulation environment
env = gym.make('FetchReach-v1')
observation = env.reset()

# Take a random action to see the robot move
action = env.action_space.sample()
observation, reward, done, info = env.step(action)

print("Observation:", observation)
